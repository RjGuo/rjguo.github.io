Efficient Ab initio Method for Calculations of Electron-Defect Scattering

Efficient Ab initio Method for Calculations of Electron-Defect Scattering

Introduction

As is known to all, electron-phonon interactions govern charge carriers dynamics at room temperature, where they play intrinsic roles in limiting carrier mobility.On the other hand, just like phonons, defects in materials can also scatter or capture charge carriers. They become significant for carrier dynamics at low temperature,where phonons are frozen out.

However, first-principle calculations of electron-defect interactions are still in their infancy. Those calculations need large supercells to obtain the electron wave function and defect perturbation potentials, which may lead huge computational cost. Here, I’d like to introduce a more efficient scheme to compute the electron-defect scattering matrix elements. This approach is firstly proposed by Bernardi’s group at Caltech, and it dose not require the wave functions of large supercells, which dramatically accelerates the calculations.

Methodology

The electron-defect scattering matrix elements $M_{n^{\prime} k^{\prime} , nk}$ encode the probability amplitude from the unperturbed state $|nk\rangle$ to $|n^\prime k^\prime \rangle$ due to the perturbation potential $\Delta V_{e-d}$ from a defect:
$$
M_{n^{\prime} k^{\prime} , nk} = \langle n^\prime k^\prime | \Delta V_{e-d} | nk\rangle
$$
